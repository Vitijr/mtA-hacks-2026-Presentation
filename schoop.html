Project Pitch

Schoop

A smarter way to schedule office hours.

Have you ever needed help from a professor, but the office hours didn’t work with your schedule?

Or maybe you sent an email asking for help and had to wait hours or even days for a reply. This is a common problem for students and professors in universities.

Press → or click Next to continue.

Problem

Today, organizing office hours or extra help sessions is often inefficient for both students and professors.

For students

  • Office hours conflict with classes, jobs, or commuting times.
  • Uncertainty about when a professor is actually available.
  • Slow back-and-forth over email just to ask a question.

For professors

  • No clear picture of when most students are free.
  • Overcrowded office hours at some times, empty ones at others.
  • Lots of individual emails to coordinate extra help sessions.

The result: overcrowded office hours, missed opportunities for help, and a lot of unnecessary coordination.

Solution — Schoop

To solve this problem, we built Schoop — a scheduling platform designed to simplify communication between students and teachers.

Smart scheduling polls

Professors create time slots for specific courses, and students quickly mark what works for them.

Instant majority view

Schoop automatically aggregates responses to show which times work best for the majority of students.

Less email, more help

Instead of long email chains, students and professors align on office hours in a single, shared view.

How it works

Professors

  1. Log in using their university email.
  2. Select the course and create available time slots.
  3. Share the Schoop poll link with their students.

Students

  1. Log in with their school email.
  2. Choose the time slots that work best for them.
  3. See how classmates responded and view the final schedule.

Behind the scenes, Schoop aggregates every response and generates a clear schedule that highlights the best-fit times.

Key features

Multiple teachers

Support for many professors using the platform at the same time.

Multiple courses

Each course has its own schedule and availability poll.

Live vote counts

Students can see how many classmates selected each time slot.

Final schedule view

Once responses are in, everyone can see the final schedule based on the teacher’s selected availability.

Technology

Schoop is deliberately lightweight, using tools that many universities already rely on.

Frontend

  • JavaScript-powered web interface.
  • Responsive layout designed for both laptops and projectors.

Backend

  • Google Apps Script as the application layer.
  • JSON-based HTTP APIs for communication with the frontend.

Data

  • Google Sheets used as a lightweight, structured database.
  • Easy to inspect, export, and share schedule data.

Impact

With Schoop, professors can organize office hours more efficiently, and students can easily find help sessions that actually fit their schedules.

For professors

  • Office hours that align with when students are truly available.
  • Fewer emails and manual scheduling headaches.

For students

  • Easier access to academic support.
  • Less time spent coordinating, more time spent learning.

Closing

Our goal is simple: make academic support easier to organize, easier to access, and more effective for both students and professors.
